The Crisis

Why this matters.
Why right now.

Every day, experienced professionals retire without documentation of what they truly knew. Industries transform faster than knowledge can transfer. Mentorship — the primary vehicle for passing wisdom — remains radically unequal, available only to those lucky enough to be near the right people.

Meanwhile, journalism — the discipline best equipped to elicit and preserve human experience — is collapsing as an industry. Its practitioners are becoming homeless just as their skills are needed most. These two crises share one solution. EdTerm is building it.

The Method

How wisdom becomes data
— and data becomes guidance.

EdTerm is not an AI that invents advice. It is a system built on real human experience, collected with discipline, preserved with care, and made accessible to anyone who needs it.

01

Train the Collectors

University journalism programs are restructured around Wisdom Journalism — training students to elicit, structure, and preserve lived human experience as Wisdom Nodes.

02

Build the Archive

Wisdom Nodes are collected from people across industries, geographies, and life stages — with rigorous ethical consent and institutional stewardship through universities.

03

Make It Findable

The Human Wisdom Database finds patterns across thousands of real experiences and surfaces what people in structurally similar situations actually did — and what happened next.

The Unit

What is a Wisdom Node?

A Wisdom Node is the fundamental unit of the Human Wisdom Database — a structured narrative artifact that captures lived experience in a form that is both deeply human and computationally retrievable.

Layer 01

Contextual Frame

Who this person was, what world they were operating in, what the stakes were. The metadata that allows future users to find relevant nodes.

Layer 02

The Threshold Moment

The specific decision, crisis, or turning point at the heart of the story — when something was truly at stake.

Layer 03

The Hidden Interior

What they were actually experiencing psychologically. The fears beneath the surface. The most valuable — and most undocumented — layer.

Layer 04

The Navigation

How they moved through it — specific actions taken, mistakes made, reversals navigated, resources drawn upon.

Layer 05

The Residue

What the experience left behind. What changed in how they see themselves and the world. The transformation that makes it wisdom.

Layer 06

Transferable Insight

What someone navigating a similar situation could actually use — developed collaboratively, never forced or prescribed.

The Vision

What this means
for the future.

EdTerm is not a product launch. It is the beginning of a new kind of knowledge infrastructure — one that treats lived human experience as the resource it actually is.

Phase One — Foundation

Wisdom Journalism Pilot Programs

Partner with 2–3 university journalism programs to develop and validate the Wisdom Journalism curriculum. Train the first cohort of Wisdom Node collectors. Build the ethical and consent frameworks that will govern the database.

Phase Two — Collection

The Human Wisdom Database Grows

Universities become active stewards — collecting from faculty, alumni, and communities. Professional schools replace static case studies with living nodes from practitioners. The database achieves the scale needed for meaningful pattern recognition.

Phase Three — Access

Mentorship Without Borders

Anyone navigating a consequential decision has access to the distilled experience of thousands of people who have navigated what they are navigating. The guidance gap closes.

The Mission

Whose wisdom gets preserved.
Who gets access.

The wisdom that disappears fastest belongs to people whose lives contain the most hard-won lessons — those who have navigated poverty, illness, discrimination, migration, and exclusion without documentation or recognition.

EdTerm is an act of epistemic justice. Your experience counts as knowledge. It deserves to outlast you and reach people you will never meet who are navigating what you have navigated.
