EdTerm Framework

Learning is the development
of a person — not the
accumulation of information.

The Learning Identity System is EdTerm's framework for tracking how individuals adapt, decide, grow, and build identity through real-world experience over time — not just what they know at a fixed point in time.

Pre-Product · Seeking Pilot Partners & Academic Collaborators

What It Is

A human-centered model
of lifelong learning.

The current education system measures what a person knows at a fixed point in time. It does not measure who they are becoming. A grade captures a moment. A test score captures recall under pressure. A transcript captures completion. None of these capture the qualities that actually predict success in work and life.

The Learning Identity System changes that. By combining experiential learning, mentorship, reflection, and behavioral pattern analysis, it creates a dynamic, living record of a learner's growth — tracking adaptive capacity, decision-making patterns, curiosity, resilience, and values development across years, not semesters.

Employers consistently report that academic credentials are weak predictors of job performance. What they actually need to know — how someone thinks under pressure, how they adapt when plans fail, how they collaborate — is invisible in a traditional transcript. The Learning Identity System closes that gap.

Why It Matters

What gets measured
gets developed.

Traditional systems were built to sort people efficiently, not to understand them deeply. The Learning Identity System measures what actually matters for a person's long-term growth and capability.

The Framework

Five core components
working together.

01

The Learner Profile

A dynamic living document that captures who the learner is becoming across multiple dimensions — updated continuously over years, not at semester intervals.

02

Experiential Learning Capture

Records learning that happens outside a classroom — internships, community projects, personal challenges, creative work, travel, and entrepreneurship. Most of this currently disappears from any educational record.

03

Behavioral Pattern Engine

An AI layer that identifies patterns the learner themselves may not consciously recognize — not surveillance, but interpretation. Inputs for mentors and for the learner's own self-awareness.

04

Mentorship Integration

The system only functions with humans in the loop. The mentor relationship is not a feature of the system — it is the heart of it. Technology makes that relationship more informed, not replaced.

05

Portable Learning Identity

Owned by the learner and portable across institutions, employers, and life stages. A Learning Identity that disappears at graduation is not a Learning Identity — it is just a different kind of transcript.
